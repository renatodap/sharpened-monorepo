## The Feel Sharper Sleep Protocol

### Day 1: Reset Your Light Exposure

Your circadian rhythm is controlled by light. Most men get this backwards—too little light during the day, too much at night.

**Morning (Within 30 minutes of waking):**
- Get 10-15 minutes of direct sunlight exposure
- No sunglasses during this window
- If cloudy, use a 10,000 lux light therapy lamp for 20 minutes

**Evening (2 hours before bed):**
- Dim all lights to 50% or less
- Use blue light blocking glasses if using screens
- Install f.lux or similar software on devices

**Why this works:** Light is the primary zeitgeber (time cue) for your circadian clock. Morning light advances your circadian phase, while evening light delays it.

### Day 2: Optimize Your Sleep Environment

Your bedroom should be a cave: cool, dark, and quiet.

**Temperature:** 65-68°F (18-20°C)
- Your core body temperature naturally drops 1-2°F before sleep
- A cool environment facilitates this process

**Darkness:** Blackout everything
- Blackout curtains or eye mask
- Cover or remove all LED lights
- Use red light bulbs for any necessary lighting

**Sound:** Consistent background noise
- Earplugs or white noise machine
- Aim for 40-50 decibels maximum

### Day 3: Master Your Pre-Sleep Routine

The 90 minutes before bed are critical. This is when you signal to your body that it's time to wind down.

**90 minutes before bed:**
- Stop all work and stimulating activities
- Begin dimming lights throughout your home
- No more food or alcohol

**60 minutes before bed:**
- Take a hot shower or bath (raises then drops core temperature)
- Practice 4-7-8 breathing: Inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8
- Read fiction or listen to calm music

**30 minutes before bed:**
- All screens off (or with blue light blockers)
- Keep bedroom temperature at 65-68°F
- Use magnesium glycinate (200-400mg) if needed

## The Science Behind Sleep Optimization

### Sleep Architecture Matters

Quality sleep isn't just about duration—it's about cycling properly through sleep stages:

- **Stage 1-2 (Light Sleep):** 45-55% of total sleep
- **Stage 3 (Deep Sleep):** 15-20% of total sleep  
- **REM Sleep:** 20-25% of total sleep

Deep sleep is when your body repairs tissue and consolidates memories. REM sleep is crucial for cognitive function and emotional regulation.

### Circadian Rhythm Optimization

Your circadian rhythm is a 24-hour biological clock that regulates:
- Core body temperature
- Hormone production (cortisol, melatonin, growth hormone)
- Alertness and cognitive performance

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Mistakes">
- Taking melatonin at the wrong time or dose
- Exercising too close to bedtime (within 3 hours)
- Inconsistent sleep/wake times on weekends
- Using alcohol as a sleep aid (it fragments sleep)
</Callout>

## Measuring Your Progress

Track these metrics to optimize your protocol:

**Subjective measures:**
- Morning energy level (1-10 scale)
- Time to fall asleep
- Number of wake-ups
- Morning mood

**Objective measures (if using a tracker):**
- Sleep efficiency (time asleep / time in bed)
- Deep sleep percentage
- REM sleep percentage
- Heart rate variability (HRV)
